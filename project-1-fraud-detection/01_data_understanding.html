Dataset Overview¶

The PaySim Fraud Detection dataset is a synthetic mobile money transactions dataset sourced from Kaggle.

It is designed to replicate real-world financial transaction behavior, including transfers, cash-outs, and payments, making it suitable for fraud detection analysis.

  • The dataset simulates realistic transaction patterns
  • It includes both normal and fraudulent transaction scenarios
  • It is widely used for building and evaluating fraud detection models

Business Questions & Answers¶

How many transactions are fraud? There are 1,142 fraudulent transactions in the dataset.

Which transaction types are most risky? Fraud occurs only in TRANSFER and CASH_OUT transactions, making them the most risky transaction types. No fraud was observed in PAYMENT, CASH_IN or DEBIT transactions.

Are high-value transactions more likely to be fraud? Yes, high-value transactions are more likely to be fraudulent. Fraud cases tend to involve significantly larger transaction amounts, with many extreme high-value outliers.

Are fraud cases concentrated in certain patterns? Yes, fraud is highly concentrated in specific patterns. It occurs only in TRANSFER and CASH_OUT transactions. These patterns suggest fraudsters focus on moving and withdrawing funds Other transaction types show no fraud activity
